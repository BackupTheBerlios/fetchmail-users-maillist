Quoting from Matthias Andree's mail on Fri, Nov 19, 2010:
> I wonder, is this of practical relevance?  Are there servers that
> support STARTTLS without advertising it?
> 
> Of course one might argue that if the user tells us to do STARTTLS, we
> should rather than give the coward. I'll take the patch.

I can now think of one instance where this is relevant. As per RFC
2595 and the comments in imap.c, in a man-in-the-middle attack, the
attacker may alter the unencrypted CAPABILITY string.

Now, if the attacker suppresses STARTTLS support from there, fetchmail
will give a socket error directly when encryption is required. If this
happens repeatedly, this will lead to a denial-of-service attack.

That patch will avoid this. Thanks for taking it.
