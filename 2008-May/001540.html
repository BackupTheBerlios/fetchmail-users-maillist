On Thu, May 22, 2008 at 2:25 AM,  <avi at phpclub.org> wrote:
> Hello,
>
> I am using fetchmail installed as a service with cygwin.
> It has the deamon option specified to get messages every 30 seconds.
>
> I am receving big files via mail (sometimes over 40mb and in rear cases even
> 90-110mb attachment).
>
> Question is simple: Can there be a case where fetchmail beings to withdraw the
> big attachment message and it takes it over 30 seconds to fetch the message
> (since its big), and somehow it screws the download when another instance is
> being ran ?

No, because the daemon interval is the sleep interval between runs.
Using the daemon mode ensures you'll never try to run 2 copies at once
(and there is locking to ensure that even if you were running it from
cron that wouldn't happen).

Two things to be aware of:

1) 30 seconds could be bordering on the abusive and some ISPs may take
action against you - 2 minutes is the shortest interval I'd suggest
against a mail server you don't own.  If your email is really that
time critical then maybe it's time to install an all SMTP solution.
2) There is a history of fetchmail not behaving "normally" under
Cygwin - search the list archive for details.
