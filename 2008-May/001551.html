On Tue, May 27, 2008 at 5:19 PM, Globe Trotter <itsme_410 at yahoo.com> wrote:
> Hi,
>
> I am here on a setup which needs kerberos 5 tickets for me to read e-mail from a POP3 server.
>
> I set up my .fetchmailrc as follows:
>
> set logfile ~/fetchmail.log
> poll pop-3.domain.edu with proto KPOP uidl
> user 'trotter' there with password 'TROTTER' is trotter here warnings 3600
> mda 'procmail -f-'
> keep
>
>
> The primary issue is that the POP server goes by the name of pop-1, pop-2, pop-3 etc. which can change at random without notice.

That sounds like an unsolvable problem to be honest.  If they are
going to do that, the only option you've got is IMAP.  POP3 doesn't
have a concept of a message status, all tracking is done client side.
If they support IMAP then you've got a hope, but otherwise I suspect
you're SOL.

If all they're doing is changing the name of the POP server, but it's
the same server, then hacking your fetchids may work.  However, I
don't believe that they are common across POP servers.
