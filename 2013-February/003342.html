> 
> Here's the relevant output:
> 
>> fetchmail: 6.3.18 querying imap.googlemail.com (protocol IMAP) at Fri
>> Feb  1 19:05:35 2013: poll started
>> reading message [MY_EMAIL]@googlemail-imap.l.google.com:1 of 1 (2113
>> header octets)
>> fetchmail: nameserver failure while looking for 'noreply.github.com'
>> during poll of imap.googlemail.com: No address associated with hostname
>> [TRIMMED]

You must upgrade your operating system.

Your libc is either not providing getaddrinfo, so that libesmtp's
(obsolete) replacement is being used, or your libc is returning a
non-POSIX error code that decodes to "No address associated with
hostname", (EAI_NODATA), when it should be returning EAI_NONAME (which
FreeBSD 7.3, Solaris 10, and Ubuntu Linux 10.04 do - the "oldest"
systems that I could find).

Upgrade your OS and after that, install fetchmail 6.3.24, then you
should not need to hack /etc/hosts.
