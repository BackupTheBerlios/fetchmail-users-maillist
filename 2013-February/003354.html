You get it because traditionally that header (X-IMAP) is used by
certain combinations of software to avoid issues. Fetchmail is logging
the fact - changing your log level may stop it logging that, but it
may also stop it logging other details.

>   the 13-line X-IMAP header is created by alpine when I save
>   a mail to a new folder, but in this case I don't see any reason why
>   it could be there.

Have you checked with the developer of "alpine"? Are you sure that
Alpine is creating those headers? Have you tried a different mail
client?

>   An other problem is that fetchmail repeats the "retained" message
>   forever, every 20 seconds, and the only way I found to avoid
>   filling the log was to remove the corresponding entry from .fetchmailrc.

Presumably then you've got fetchmail checking every 20 seconds? Can
you provide the information listed in section G3 of the FAQ
(http://www.fetchmail.info/fetchmail-FAQ.html#G3).
