On Sun, Apr 26, 2009 at 11:26:19AM +0200, Andrea R. wrote:
> Hello,
> 
> I have succeeded in getting fetchmail (6.3.6) to talk to a POP3+TLS
> mailserver that uses a self-signed certificate, by passing the
> "sslfingerprint" option.
[snip]
> fetchmail: Server CommonName mismatch: localhost.localdomain != mail.***.com
[snip]
> These warnings are not being suppressed by the '-s' option, which I'm
> already using. Is there a way to get rid of them, short of redirecting
> stderr to /dev/null, which would prevent me from seeing all other
> warnings?

Well, it's not just a self-signed certificate, it's a self-signed
certificate for a... let's say, too generic a hostname :)

There are two things you can do:

1. Try something like:

   fetchmail ... 2>&1 | fgrep -ve 'Server CommonName mismatch'

...so that you filter out this line and this line alone.

2. Talk to the people at ***.com and get them to issue another SSL
   certificate for their server; it may still be self-signed
   (as long as you find a way to get its fingerprint in a secure way),
   but it really ought to be issued for CN=mail.***.com.

G'luck,
Peter
