More than half the time I get AUTHFAIL from the server.  

Tech support at the server says they are not having any problems at their end.

Here are a couple of lines from mail.log:
Oct 12 16:00:29 pti-server fetchmail[3773]: Authorization failure on admin at homapump.com@pop.homapump.com
Oct 12 16:00:29 pti-server fetchmail[3773]: Query status=3 (AUTHFAIL)
Oct 12 16:00:35 pti-server fetchmail[3773]: Authorization failure on acctg1 at homapump.com@pop.homapump.com
Oct 12 16:00:35 pti-server fetchmail[3773]: Query status=3 (AUTHFAIL)

And when it fails I get this email subject:
fetchmail authentication failed on ed@<mydomain>.com at pop.<mydomain>.com
****** body ************
Fetchmail could not get mail from ed@<mydomain>.com at pop.<mydomain>.com.

The attempt to get authorization failed.
Since we have already succeeded in getting authorization for this
connection, this is probably another failure mode (such as busy server)
that fetchmail cannot distinguish because the server didn't send a useful
error message.

However, if you HAVE changed your account details since starting the
fetchmail daemon, you need to stop the daemon, change your configuration
of fetchmail, and then restart the daemon.

The fetchmail daemon will continue running and attempt to connect
at each cycle.  No future notifications will be sent until service
is restored.
-- 
The Fetchmail Daemon
****** end body ********

The doubling up of "@<mydomain>" above seems odd.

When this error occurs it happens for everyone.  When it works, I get this message.
When it works, I get this email subject:
fetchmail authentication OK on ed@<mydomain>.com at pop.<mydomain>.com
