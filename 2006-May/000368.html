Andres,

Rob didn't know about the "set daemon 60" option which sends output to
/dev/null. You can override that option with with "-d0" or "--daemon 0"
on the command line, or, if you want to leave fetchmail in daemon mode,
the "--nodetach" (-N) option keeps output on your screen while in daemon
mode. (With syslog enabled, i. e. omitting the --nosyslog, the mail log
file, often /var/log/mail or /var/log/maillog, will contain that
information -- perhaps you need to tell syslog via /etc/syslog.conf to
capture mail.debug logging.)

Rob,

I have adjusted the G3 item of the FAQ <http://www.fetchmail.info/fetchmail-FAQ.html#G3>
to suggest "--nosyslog --nodetach -vvv"
(option merging is safe BTW since fetchmail ships a copy of GNU getopt,
so the comand line capabilities do not depend on system libraries).
