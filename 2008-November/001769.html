Hum, I've seen Novell OES fetchmail security updates. If they haven't fixed
CVE-2008-2711 or CVE-2007-4565 yet, you might want to send a gentle request
to include those, and perhaps the two critical fixes from 6.3.9, in an
update. There were not too intrusive and the SVN repo where they can pull
from is freely accessible.

Having said that, 6.3.8 is - for the nonce - recent enough to qualify for
support on this list until I request that you try 6.3.9.

BTW, have you seen the FAQ? Particularly question and answer G3 are relevant:

http://www.fetchmail.info/fetchmail-FAQ.html#G3

> So, in their view, there is no support.   Last I checked.

I'm not a Novell customer, so I don't care. The alternative is going with
the latest version(s) that are supported on the list, these today are
6.3.9, and 6.3.8 to some extent.

I for one don't have the resources to keep a zoo of old distributions
around; I rather have to see to replacing my development computer in the
not too distant future. /Usually/ distributions aren't the culprit if you
compile a recent version of fetchmail yourself. The quick starting point,
barring special requirements is the usual "./configure --with-ssl && make"
as unprivileged user and then "make install" as super user.

/var/log/mail*, perhaps /var/log/warn for a quick glance. If there are any

are the ones to deal with first.
