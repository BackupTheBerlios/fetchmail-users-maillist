On Sat, Apr 17, 2010 at 22:43, Robert Holtzman <holtzm at cox.net> wrote:
> This one has me talking to myself. After solving my original problem  of
> connecting to two separate servers everything worked for several days. As of
> yesterday running fetchmail on my laptop gave me this:
>
> 7 messages for holtzm at pop.sonic.net (45002 octets).
> reading message holtzm at pop.sonic.net:1 of 7 (3610 octets)..fetchmail:
> connection to localhost:smtp [127.0.0.1/25] failed: Connection refused.
> fetchmail: connection to localhost:smtp [127.0.1.1/25] failed: Connection
> refused.
> fetchmail: SMTP connect to localhost failed
> fetchmail: SMTP transaction error while fetching from holtzm at pop.sonic.net
> and delivering to SMTP host localhost
> fetchmail: Query status=10 (SMTP)
> 34 messages for holtzm at pop.west.cox.net (195513 octets).
> reading message holtzm at pop.west.cox.net:1 of 34 (5734 octets)..... flushed
> reading message holtzm at pop.west.cox.net:2 of 34 (4046 octets)... flushed
> reading message holtzm at pop.west.cox.net:3 of 34 (12930 octets)............
> flushed
>
> and went on to d/l all 34 from cox.net. I copied .fetchmailrc onto my
> desktop and it connected to *both* servers and d/l'd all messages.
>
> As I posted in my previous thread, my .fetchmailrc is:
>
> poll "pop.sonic.net"
> protocol pop3 username "xxxxxx" password "xxxxxxxx"
> mimedecode #
> #
> poll "pop.west.cox.net"
> protocol pop3 username "xxxxxx" password "xxxxxxxxxx" mimedecode mda
> "/usr/bin/procmail -f -"
>
> Why does it work on one machine and not the other and on the one that fails,
> why with one server and not the other?

It fails because you're not running an SMTP server.

Take a look at the error message:

> fetchmail: connection to localhost:smtp [127.0.1.1/25] failed: Connection
> refused.
> fetchmail: SMTP connect to localhost failed
> fetchmail: SMTP transaction error while fetching from holtzm at pop.sonic.net
> and delivering to SMTP host localhost

For that account, you've left the default at delivering by the local
SMTP server, that you're not running.  Your desktop presumably has one
running.

The second account you're specified an MDA, hence why that one works.
