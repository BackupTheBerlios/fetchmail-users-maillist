RFC 5322 (the successor to 822) also states, quote:

"2.2.  Header Fields

   Header fields are lines beginning with a field name, followed by a
   colon (":"), followed by a field body, and terminated by CRLF.  A
   field name MUST be composed of printable US-ASCII characters (i.e.,
   characters that have values between 33 and 126, inclusive), except
   colon.  [...]"

Quite obviously the web.de server does *not* conform to RFC-5322, so you can't
invoke RFC-5322 here.

Only to have the next person blame fetchmail because it discards the invalid
header line? No thanks. We can't win, see below.

I'd considered a heuristic like "line with only whitespace followed by invalid
header", but even that might truncate the header prematurely...

Consider:

"Subject: blah\r\n
    \r\n
fasel\r\n"

and:

"Sender: blah at example.org\r\n
    \r\n
This is some MIME prolog and syntactically part of the body\r\n"


The former might have been the mistaken attempt at folding "Subject: blah
fasel", and the latter a b0rked template for a mail merge.  It really needs to
be refused by the upstream server. There's no way for fetchmail to disambiguate
both.

It'd be nice if someone wrote the code with proper options to have some kind of:

handle-invalid-headers: {method}

Where method is one of the following:

- skip-message: does what we have now,

- pass-invalid-message-verbatim: would ignore the corruption and disable the
check as proposed by Frédéric Marchal,

- escape-offending-line: prefix it with X-Fetchmail-Invalid-Header: (You'd get
two in this situation, the blank one and the MIME prolog line) - note this may
need proper escaping, and

- terminate-header: insert the required blank line to delineate header and body.

- drop-offending-line: would kill the invalid line as in your proposal

I'd prefer skip-messsage over escape-... over any other options.

This is a lot of work just to make up because someone else goofed up his part.
There's more important work to do in fetchmail than working around broken
servers, like bugfixing.
