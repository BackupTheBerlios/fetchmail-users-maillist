On Thu, 24 Sep 2009 09:04:06 +0530
Vikas Rawal <vikaslists at agrarianresearch.org> wrote:

> I use fetchmail to connect to gmail's pop3 server. For some time now,
> I have been having a problem that fetchmail fetches every message
> twice over. 
> 
> The relevant section of .fetchmailrc is:
> 
> set daemon 120
> 
> poll pop.gmail.com with proto pop3,
> user myusername at gmail.com is myname here options ssl sslcertck
> sslcertpath '/home/myname/certs/.certs',
> with password mypassword;
> with mda "procmail -d %T"
> 
> It does not help to add nokeep to above.
> 
> This is such a nuisance. Shall be grateful for help.

From my .fetchmailrc file:

poll pop.gmail.com with proto POP3 timeout 30 and options uidl
   user 'recent:me at gmail,com' there with password 'secret' is 'me'
   here options forcecr ssl sslcertpath '/usr/local/etc/postfix/certs/'
   sslfingerprint '92:73:17:4C:34:4B:68:F7:B2:17:71:42:0D:7F:9F:33'

I have not had any problems; then again I stay as far away
from' procmail' as humanly possible.

Did you check the settings on your GMail account. They have options on
what to do with 'POP' mail. Mine is set to delete the GMail copy after
POP.
