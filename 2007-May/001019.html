> Hi,
>
> I use:
> "poll pop.example.com with proto POP3
> user "general at example.com" there with password 'my_password' is dave here"
> which places the catch-all mail in the file /var/spool/mail/dave
>
> I need the recipients dave at example.com, john at example.com and
> shirley at example.com to be distributed to the local mailboxes of users
> dave, john and shirley.
>
> I would also like the mail to barry at example.com to remain on the ISP's
> server (if this is possible, it's not absolutely necessary).
>
> Can anyone assist me on how to achieve this?

Basically, it depends on your ISP.  If they've provided a header with
this information (often something like Envelope-to or Delivered-to)
then you can (and as Matthias said the details are in the man page).
If you're not sure, provide a sample header for an email and I'll let
you know.

If however they haven't, your out of luck.
