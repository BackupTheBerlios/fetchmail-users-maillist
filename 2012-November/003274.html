This is, arguably, the best solution - not only for this case, but also
in general, in keeping with the Unix philosophy of building a chain of
several tools, each of which does one thing and does it well.

Most people are scared of writing tools to generate config files for
other tools for a long time, until one day they decide to try it and
they find out that it's not really all that hard :)  Of course, it takes
some time and attention to detail to get various issues right - the most
important one being atomicity - either you generate a complete new
configuration or you do not modify the current configuration at all.
Still, in the end it's really not that hard, and most of the time it's
much, much easier than maintaining local patches for a multitude of
tools that don't work *quite* the way we might want them to for our
specific configuration :)
