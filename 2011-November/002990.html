You always have the option of editing the source to suit your
provider. However you then won't get any support on this list for
problems that result from doing that. However, the sample you
provided, now I look again, appears to be in the right format:

> Received: from ([74.125.xx.xx]) for <administrator at mylocaldomain.com> with MailEnable Catch-All Filter; Fri, 11 Nov 2011 15:58:56 +0100

You may find that if you configure Fetchmail such that it knows that
that IP is an alias for the server in question then it might work.
