On Sun, Nov 6, 2011 at 09:42, brian <Brian_Dorling at t-online.de> wrote:
> Hi,
> I currently have a small home server running debian. This uses fetchmail to
> get my emails and
> stores them on the server where Dovecot is running as an imap server so I
> can access my emails
> via thunderbird. This all works pretty well.
>
> I want to do the following though. When an email comes in I want to look at
> the sender and the subject
> and trigger a script on my server if certain conditions are met. Its going
> to stay pretty basic.
>
> Up till now I couldn't find out how to do this. In fetchmail, in dovecot,
> maybe with a spam filter software?
>
> I would be grateful for any tips pointing me in the right direction.

Fetchmail certainly doesn't support that, but you could certainly do
that with the likes of MIMEDefang and AMAViS, and probably any other
mail scanning platform. You could probably do it by forwarding a copy
of all email to a script of your own creation and then parsing the
email there.
