Richard Ibbotson schrieb:

> Does anyone know what this might be about ?  Tried the Google search 
> and nothing useful came back.

It's about what the error message says.
fetchmail doesn't break all of a sudden without further ado, something must
have changed in the environment.

> Ubuntu 8.04.  Fetchmail 6.3.8.  Worked fine for months on end 
> downloading mail for Kmail to read.  If I do 'fetchmail -v' then 
> everything works fine.  If I do something like 'fetchmail -d650' 
> or 'fetchmail -d0' I get the following error message....
> 
> " gethostbyname failed for localhost
> Name or service not knownCannot find my own host in hosts database to 
> qualify it!  Trying to continue with unqualified hostname.
> DO NOT report broken Received: headers, HELO/EHLO lines or similar 
> problems!  DO repair your /etc/hosts, DNS, NIS or LDAP instead.  "
> 
> Which means that no mail is downloaded.

No, it does _not_ mean that. "Trying to continue" is what it says - since
you're not using Kerberos IV, 5, ODMR or ETRN, it might work so it's worth
a go.

It means fetchmail cannot resolve your own hostname, and that's it - fix
the cause for that. Check /etc/nsswitch.conf, /etc/host.conf,
/etc/resolv.conf, /etc/hosts and whatever else may be relevant for resolving.

To find the actual non-delivery issue, try fetchmail --nodetach --nosyslog
-vv (or even -vvv) and see what you get, with or without -d.

> I've tried to flush DNS 
> with '/etc/init.d/nscd restart '  but that doesn't make any 
> difference.  Any ideas what to try next ?   Nothing was changed on 
> this machine before Fetchmail stopped working.

I don't believe the latter statement. Perhaps YOU did not change something,
or "nothing obvious changed", but something has changed (mind the wording).

Ditch (= dpkg --purge) nscd on Linux if it's a desktop.
It's no good on systems with just a few users and the glibc guys don't get
nsswitch and thereabouts right (for years) and just wastes memory and more
importantly human resources since it gets in the way.
I have yet to see a system besides Solaris that gets nsswitch right.
