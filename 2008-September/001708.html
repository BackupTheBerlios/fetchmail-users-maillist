It isn't nice to toss the mail as simple text in an encapsulating text e-mail 
because that makes it unreadable if it is more than a simple bare ASCII text 
(look at the source of your e-mails to see what I mean).

Therefore, that "envelope" implies to build a valid MIME encapsulating message 
with all the headers and MIME stuff without conflicting with any (possibly 
corrupted) MIME structure the original message may have. We must also add an 
explanation message in the multipart encapsulating message for the benefit of 
the final recipient. The explanation message along with the subject line of 
the encapsulated message must be configurable to let the site administrator 
adapt the message or its language.

It is not difficult. It just takes time :-)
