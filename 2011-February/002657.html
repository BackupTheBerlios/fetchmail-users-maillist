On Fri, Feb 18, 2011 at 22:09, Jude DaShiell <jdashiel at shellworld.net> wrote:
> Earlier I read configure -help output on current version of fetchmail and
> tried building with -with-ntlm and later with -enable-ntlm switches and
> configure complained that both of those switches are invalid.  Has -ntlm
> been removed from fetchmail or is it just built in by default without the
> need to mention it?  I'm trying to build for accessing my email on my msn
> account.

What version (number) of Fetchmail are you trying to build and on
which platform?

Checking the output from "./configure --help" (note the pair of '-'
symbols) on 6.3.9 I see the option for NTLM support is
'--enable-NTLM'. Using that, exactly as shown in the output from
"./configure --help" works.

Generally speaking everything in unix type systems (such as Linux and
BSD) is case sensitive - '--enable-ntlm' and '--enable-NTLM' are 2
different things. Similarly '-help' and '--help' are different things.
