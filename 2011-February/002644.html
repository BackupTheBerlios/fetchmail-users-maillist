For those looking for root certs...

You can always get them from the CA's themselves,
though they can prove hard to find on their sites.
You can also export certs from within some browsers.
It's simpler and more consistent to extract them from
the browser source code stash. No idea about MS's stash,
but you can get Mozilla's here:
http://mxr.mozilla.org/security/source/security/nss/lib/ckfw/builtins/certdata.txt?raw=1

CA's should generally be considered as a convenience, and
optional at that, not some golden entities entrusted with
your life. You can google that concept if you want. And then
there's all the other cert attack vectors as well. So always
configure fingerprint checking. Preferably with both SHA-1
and MD5 until the outcome of the NIST competition trickles
down. I believe fetchmail still only supports MD5.
