Maybe - that depends on how exactly your cron line looks like - can you  
show that?
I suspect there are some verbose options - and cron will dutifully mail  
the verbose output. So if you request it, you'll get it.

You can also filter particular lines out by piping fetchmail output  
through grep -v or sed (perhaps you also need a 2>&1 before the pipe  
symbol so that stderr gets filtered, too), or you can redirect fetchmail's  
output to a file, either by regular shell means (even in crontab), or  
through the logfile option (if you're using fetchmail's daemon mode on the  
crontab line).
