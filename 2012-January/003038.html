On Mon, Jan 30, 2012 at 18:11, skull <skull17 at gmx.ch> wrote:
> Am 30.01.2012 19:04, schrieb Rob MacGregor:
>> "multidrop" is where a single mailbox receives email for more than one
>> address. For instance, where your provider gives you only a single
>> mailbox, but allows you to create a number of "aliases" that all
>> deliver to the same mailbox.
>>
> does that even matter in my case? the mail gets delivered to the MTA in the
> last line anyway.
> or am i missing something here?

If the mailbox receives email for multiple accounts it matters.
Without correct mutlidrop support all the email from *that mailbox*
will be sent to the same recipient.

If however you have one mailbox per recipient address then it doesn't
matter since your mailboxes are all singledrop.
