Bill Jastram schrieb am 2008-06-23:

> I have been using Fetchmail & Mutt for many years, but just recently Fetchmail ceased to retrieve 
> mail.  I tested it with fetchmail -c and got the following error:
> 
> fetchmail: Server certificate verification error: unable to get local issuer 
> certificate
> fetchmail: Server certificate verification error: certificate not trusted
> 16 messages for xxxx at mail.comcast.net (59239 octets).
> 
> What could have happened to cause this interruption in email retrieval?

Hi Bill,

likely a "change" (including expiry) in SSL certificates of the upstream
server or the certification chain, or damage to the directory/file of
trusted root issuers, or their recent enabling of SSL/TLS when it was
disabled before; fetchmail 6.3.8 in verbose mode should print more
detailed information so you can see better what's going on.

What fetchmail says is basically that nobody told it (or the OpenSSL
library for that matter) that the issuer of the upstream server's
certificate is trustworthy - the latter usually happens by tossing the
/issuer/ intermediate (if applicable) and root certificate(s) into the
CApath (sslcertpath in fetchmail), often /usr/ssl/certs, /etc/ssl/certs,
or thereabouts (after installation, c_rehash /etc/ssl/certs, or similar,
has to be executed to generate links).
