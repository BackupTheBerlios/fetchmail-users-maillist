Hi Perry,

thanks for the report.  This should be harmless in that fetchmail will use its
own MD5 implementation instead.
  It is often a hint that the header file is broken and doesn't pull in its
dependencies, i. e. lacks #include lines.
  Do you have a md5.h in /usr/local/include or other local paths that didn't get
installed by the operating system?

For a detailed analysis, I need more information, so please send to me directly
(off-list):

1. a copy of your config.log file. Feel free to compress it with gzip, bzip2 or
lzma or xz.

Now you need to figure which md5.h got picked up, try reading config.log, or
"locate md5.h"

2. the output of: rpm -qf /usr/include/md5.h (or whichever md5.h was picked up
-- check config.log and the locate output)

3. the /usr/include/md5.h (or whichever md5.h was picked up -- check config.log
and the locate output).

4. the output of: gcc -### fetchmail.c

That together should allow me to figure what's going on.  I may have more questions.

Best regards
Matthias
