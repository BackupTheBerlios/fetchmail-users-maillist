On Sun, Dec 12, 2010 at 14:50, <skip at pobox.com> wrote:
>
> I'm trying to help out a SpamBayes user on Windows who is trying to grab
> mail from Windows Live via SSL (from pop.live.com:995).  The SpamBayes
> package doesn't do POP3S correctly, so he's having no luck pumping his email
> through SpamBayes via that connection.
>
> I believe he should be able to use fetchmail to grab his mail (that's how I
> get mail from Gmail), but I don't know what to tell him to insert between
> fetchmail and the SpamBayes POP3 proxy on his Windows machine.  Any
> suggestions?

It looks like your out of luck.  Fetchmail won't do anything for you
that your mail client won't since SpamBayes is just a POP3 proxy.
That means that it needs to be SSL aware since it is doing the actual
communication with the remote POP server.  As it's over 5 years old I
wouldn't expect to see SSL support in it any time soon.

A few seconds with Google (SpamBayes SSL) found various guides to
using stunnel to solve your problem, including
http://www.peterbe.com/plog/blogitem-20031117-1600
