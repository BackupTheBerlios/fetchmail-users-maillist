The ssl-related options in fetchmail <= 6.x.y are awkward to use.

There are two features to consider:

- whether a connection is legacy SSL-wrapped (i. e. after connecting the
sockets the SSL negotiation starts right away) or needs in-band
negotiation of SSL/TLS ("STARTTLS" or "STLS" in IMAP/POP3,
respectively); SSL-wrapped uses port 993/995 for IMAP/POP3 as opposed to
the regular ports 143 and 110 for STARTTLS.

- which SSL protocols to negotiate.

For all practical purposes, these are independent of one another, but
fetchmail lumps "TLS is for STARTTLS and SSL implies legacy wrapper
mode" together.

"sslproto ssl23" effectively disables TLS negotiation in versions <=
6.x.y, so unless you also use an "ssl" option, this _disables_ SSL/TLS.

It's counterintuitive and will be fixed in 7.0.

Hope it's clearer now.
