Yeah that looks like it was configured with socks support.  Having
myself dug through the documentation and FAQ it appears that there is
socks support after all (it's not documented anywhere except the FAQ):

http://www.fetchmail.info/fetchmail-FAQ.html#K1

Rebuild fetchmail without the socks support.  It looks like fetchmail
will automatically use socks if you build it with socks support -
there's no runtime switch.  (At least that's what a read of the source
tells me)

So, in short, while you didn't intend to configure fetchmail to use
your socks server, you did by configuring socks support into fetchmail
(and I'm guessing defining some environment variables or putting
entries in socks.conf).
