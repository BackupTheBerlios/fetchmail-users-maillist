> Our web hosting ISP made some changes to their hosting, and it wrecked 
> our downloading of email from that ISP to our mail server. The ISP had 
> been using a "self made certificate" or something similar, that had 
> expired, that ended up preventing us from donloading email, and they did 
> a (apparently cheap and nasty) patch, that led from one error that we 
> received, to another.

According to the message below...

> "fetchmail: awakened at Mon Dec 29 11:40:41 2008
> fetchmail: Server CommonName mismatch: plesk3.distributeit.com.au != 
> mail.busby.net
> fetchmail: sleeping at Mon Dec 29 11:40:42 2008 for 300 seconds"

...the current setup is that the name "mail.busby.net" points to a server
that thinks of itself as "plesk3.distributeit.com.au".  Both of those
names resolve to the same IP address - 114.31.74.40.

Apparently, until now the ISP has had mail.busby.net on a separate
IP address with a certificate issued for the name "mail.busby.net".
Now, the certificate's common name says "plesk3.distributeit.com.au" -
which would be just fine if the client thought it was connecting to
plesk3.distributeit.com.au.

So the solution - or the dirty hack that would let you download your
e-mail messages for the present - is very simple: change your fetchmail
configuration to "poll plesk3.distributeit.com.au" instead of
"poll mail.busby.net", and fetchmail will accept the certificate.
After that, talk to your ISP about a certificate issued to the name of
"mail.busby.net".

Hope that helps.
