> What exactly would be cool? Blocking certificates with MD5
> signatures when fetchmail is verifying SSL certificates?

Oh, no not that, of course if the cert is valid, it's valid, no
matter the type of hash in the cert that was signed.

The world is moving (has moved) to signatures over SHA1
and for fingerprinting in general, because MD5 is completely,
real world, in the wild, broken.

(And for that matter, SHA1 is headed that way on paper,
but the replacement, SHA-3, isn't here yet. So it's SHA1
for now.)

Fetchmail 'fingerprint' option does not support checking
fingerprints with SHA1. It really should.

Prior thread[s] on this list within the past year or two
began to suggest and actually detail how to option,
parse and integrate SHA1 into fetchmail. Talk/work
apparently stalled before reaching coding phase.

Is there a ticket system where this could be lodged
as a todo item?
