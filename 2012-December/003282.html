On Fri, Dec 7, 2012 at 7:38 PM, Hendrik Haddorp <hendrik.haddorp at gmx.net> wrote:
> Hi,
> I see a bit of a strange behavior of fetchmail. My .fetchmailrc file
> contains something like:
> poll pop.gmx.net service 995 proto POP3 ....

Something like? Exactly what does it contain?

> When I invoke fetchmail in verbose mode I see this in the log:
>     Dec  7 20:25:01 mail fetchmail[29202]: 6.3.21 querying pop.gmx.net
> (protocol POP3) at Fri 07 Dec 2012 08:25:01 PM CET: poll started
>     Dec  7 20:25:01 mail fetchmail[29202]: Trying to connect to <<IPv6
> address of my server>>/995...connection failed.
>     Dec  7 20:25:01 mail fetchmail[29202]: connection to pop.gmx.net:995
> [<<IPv6 address of my server>>/995] failed: Connection refused.
>     Dec  7 20:25:01 mail fetchmail[29202]: Trying to connect to
> 212.227.17.185/995...connected.
>
> With <<IPv6 address of my server>> being the static IPv6 address of my
> server. So fetchmail first tries to connect to my own server instead of
> pop.gmx.net, fails, and then tries the real address. As if the resolution of
> pop.gmx.net would return my own address. However when I invoke "host
> pop.gmx.net" I get:
>     pop.gmx.net has address 212.227.17.169
>     pop.gmx.net has address 212.227.17.185

Check your local hosts file and your resolver priorities (how you do
that depends on your OS). I'm guessing that something in your resolver
path is mapping pop.gmx.net to your local IPv6 address (there's
nothing in fetchmail that could do that).
