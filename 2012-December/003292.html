On 14.12.2012 16:30, Peter Hartman wrote:
> Hi Folks:
> 
> I'm trying to abuse fetchmail to be an e-mail notifier, much as
> reported here[1].  Roughly, I run fetchmail --fetch --verbose |
> myscript.sh, where myscript.sh parses the output and updates xsetroot
> -name "" with the number of messages.  I've been able to get it to
> play nice with EXPUNGE, such that if I access my gmail account via the
> gmail interface and archive a message out of my INBOX, fetchmail /
> myscript.sh will be aware of this.  And, of course, fetchmail /
> myscript.sh are also aware of new messages when they arrive.  However,
> there is one piece of the puzzle that is missing:
> 
> If I access my gmail account and READ a new message, no notification
> is sent to fetchmail / myscript.sh.  So it will continue to think that
> there are, e.g., 13 messages (12 dj vus) pour me at gmail.com dans
> imap.gmail.com even though, in point of fact, there are 13 messages
> (13 dj vus).  So, is there a trick?

Salut Peter,

Unfortunately, IMAP does not have such a thing - there is no "email was
read" or "\Recent flag unseen" notification in IMAP.

The server will, however, leave IDLE mode every 30 min or so, and
fetchmail will leave it a bit earlier, so you should get those counts
updated every ~28 min or so.

Else the only way of updating the counts in order to detect "mail was
read" updates is not using the --idle mode, at the expense of traffic.

There may be an extension in one of the RFCs that I am currently unaware
of that sends flag updates (like \Recent or \Seen) - tell me the RFC
number if there is such a thing -, but that would still mean that code
needed to be written and debugged, and it would not get integrated into
fetchmail 6.3.X - but have to wait until a fetchmail 7.0.X version.


Else I wonder what is so important to know that one of the messages got
read... number cosmetics :)

HTH
Matthias
