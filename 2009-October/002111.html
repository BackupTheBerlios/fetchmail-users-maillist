I suggest maildrop instead. procmail appears unmaintained, does not log  
mail delivery in default settings and has an awkward "if one rule fails to  
deliver, just use the next one" fallthrough error handling unless you  
override this (which is long-winded and makes your .procmailrc awkward to  
read). If you must continue to use procmail, do enable logging so you know  
where procmail stuffs your messages. Check the LOGFILE option.

I'm not sure I understand (a) the GMail option and (b) your issue.
What do you want to happen?
What have you done to achieve this (your .fetchmailrc without passwords  
surely helps here - you'd offered that)?
What happens instead?

Generally, see <http://www.fetchmail.info/fetchmail-FAQ.html#G3> for what  
you need to provide.

If you want old and read mail to be removed from the server, run  
"fetchmail --flush".

If you want to remove mail from the server after fetchmail has succeeded  
in retrieving and delivering it (which hinges on the reliability
of procmail, meaning that you may go on an expedition to find out where  
procmail has stuffed your mail), DO NOT set the "keep" option.

It is recommended to use "uidl" with POP3 accounts.
