Anyway, IMO the current behaviour is not that bad.

> The problem with any such changes to fetchmail is that modules such as  
> report.c are lightly documented and often used in arcane undocumented  
> ways, interspersed with ad-hoc code all over the place, and sometimes 
> code was not moved into a function, but copied and pasted. I attribute 
> this to the bazaar-style coding that was en vogue when Eric S. Raymond 
> hacked fetchmail and wrote his Cathedral and Bazaar essay (it would be 
> sensible to follow up why a bazaar gives you a lump of features, but not 
> a consistent piece in one cast).
>
> It often made the code harder to maintain, and before such features can 
> be implemented, you need to read major parts of the code to see where 
> else you'll find something that influences your current task, and that's 
> also why fetchmail progresses rather slowly WRT bugfixing and 
> particularly feature additions.
