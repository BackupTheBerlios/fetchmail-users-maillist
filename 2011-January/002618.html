Hi Martin,

--- On Sat, 22/1/11, Martin Koeppe <mkoeppe at gmx.de> wrote:

> The man page says:
> > Fetchmail's method of sending bounces due to errors or
> spam-blocking and spam bounces requires that port 25 of
> localhost be available for sending mail via SMTP.
> 
> I think the 2 points above should be made more explicit in
> the man pages, e.g. when describing "set (no) bounce",
> "smtphost", "esmtpname" and/or "esmtppassword".

Please note that the 'smtphost' and other options are related to acceptance of local mails. This means that 'smtphost' is only guaranteed to accept mails for local users and for the postmaster.

It cannot be assumed that 'smtphost' is guaranteed to relay the bounce mails even when 'esmtpname' and 'esmtppassword' are specified. For all you know, the 'smtphost' might not even be connected to the internet even indirectly.

Yes, fetchmail's bounce behaviour requires more work. However, that is not going to be trivial as any implicit assumptions are only going to muddle the issue further.
