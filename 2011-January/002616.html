Hi Sunil,

On Sat, 22 Jan 2011, Sunil Shetye wrote:

> Fetchmail does have several problems related to bouncing:
>
> - fetchmail does not use smtphost for bouncing mails. This is 
> because the mails are being relayed to the original sender and 
> fetchmail is not sure whether the smtphost is open to relaying. So, 
> for bounce messages, fetchmail always uses localhost.
>
> - postmaster option is overloaded. It is being used as a last-resort 
> address and for other internal messages when connected to 'smtphost' 
> and for bounce messages when connected to 'localhost'. The fetchmail 
> user has to ensure that the postmaster is acceptable on both 
> 'smtphost' and 'localhost'.

thanks for this explanation. I also wondered about a postmaster 
related behaviour some time ago. Now I understand.

The man page says:
> Fetchmail's method of sending bounces due to errors or spam-blocking 
> and spam bounces requires that port 25 of localhost be available for 
> sending mail via SMTP.

I think the 2 points above should be made more explicit in the man 
pages, e.g. when describing "set (no) bounce", "smtphost", "esmtpname" 
and/or "esmtppassword".
