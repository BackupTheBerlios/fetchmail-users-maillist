> On Tue, Oct 12, 2010 at 04:52:56PM +0200, Matthias Andree wrote:
>> 
>> - have fetchmail forward into a mail delivery agent (formerly procmail, now
>> discouraged, or maildrop, recommended) and configure that to feed your mail
>> through spam/virus filters before delivering it to your mailbox. See the --mda
>> option.
> 
> Could you please expand on why forwarding to procmail is discouraged?

As reiterated several times and can be found in various archives, procmail is
VERY hard to configure so that it behaves reliably rather than dumping mail into
random folders. To that end, requires the users waiving the possibility to use
:0e recipes, spamming your procmailrc with rules that cause mail deferrals with
proper exit code, and the procmailrc syntax is rather inconcise.  I've switched
many years ago and never looked back.

Also, procmail has been abandonware (unmaintained) for more than a decade.

Maildrop avoids all those problems. In case of trouble, it reliably exits with
code 75, the filter file is much more concise, and maildrop is actively
maintained.  There may be other alternatives to procmail, but maildrop is
well-established.
