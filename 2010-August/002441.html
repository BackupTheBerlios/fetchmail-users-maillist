On Mon, Aug 2, 2010 at 18:48, Depo Catcher <depocatcher at gmail.com> wrote:
>
> Well, it's 2010; common sense tells me it's a bad idea to have a file with
> dozens of plain text passwords.
> Anyways; I'm done with this.  I'm going to use a combination of pwsafe and
> truecrypt.
> Honestly I don't know why there is such hostility towards not having plain
> text passwords.

You're missing the point - this thread (and others) is about
understanding the difference between actual security, and the
appearance of security.  Encrypting passwords that have to be
automatically decrypted by any process is the latter, not the former.
The passwords held by your mail client are trivial to extract unless
you're required to unlock access to that password list every time you
launch the application (at which point it simply becomes moderately
challenging).

Better to identify the risk you're trying to offset, and then try to
solve that, than blindly assume that encryption is a magic wand ;)
