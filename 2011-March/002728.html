I know you're fond of checking finger prints on top of certificate
CommonNames and certificate chains.  I suppose Google doesn't care too
much about such practices, but they will rather make sure that if you
keep the root certificate (the one that signed the Google intermediate
CA) trusted, no disruption for the conventional certificate verification
process occurs.  Your question is actually "did Google change
certificates", and you'd better ask Google that. :)
