On Sun, Mar 6, 2011 at 18:17, James Leone <that_isridiculous at yahoo.com> wrote:
> Hi, If I wanted to use my cell phone as an email server and utilize Fetchmail to pull down emails, how do I install it?
>
> We have about 200 employees and not a lot of shelf space. The Android seems like a perfect compact solution.
>
> I also want people sitting at their desks to access their email stored on the phone with Squirrelmail and of course to send their emails out. I'm looking for MTA packages and amavis and spamassassin packages as well.

Can I (strongly) suggest that instead you look at a hosted service
then. A mobile phone is not a suitable platform for use as a mail
server (and I say that as somebody with an Android phone). At the very
least you'd need to install a full IMAP service, and a web server
there, and have a disk capable of sustaining a high write rate, which
a microSD card isn't designed for.

If you can't afford a hosted service then look at the Mini ITX
platform. It is possible to build (or buy) a server that is very
compact around that.
