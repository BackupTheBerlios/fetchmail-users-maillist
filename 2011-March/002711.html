Ok. got it! fetchmail working very nicely with gmail.

Let me recap.

1) wget http://download.berlios.de/fetchmail/fetchmail-6.3.19.tar.bz2
2) tar xf fetchmail-6.3.19.tar.bz2
3) cd fetchmail-6.3.19
	$./configure --with-ssl
	*****did not work*****
4) $sudo apt-get install libssl-dev
5) $./configure --with-ssl
	success
6) $make
7) $sudo make install
8) $vim ~/.fetchmailrc
	set postmaster "user"
	set daemon 600
	poll pop.gmail.com with proto POP3 uidl
     	user 'myname at gmail.com' there with password 'xxxx' is user here
	options ssl sslcertck
9) $fetchmail -d0 -vk pop.gmail.com
	fetched mail from google

all of which is simultaneously marvelous and quite strange.
can't believe i went so far astray with the "help" at:
http://www.axllent.org/docs/networking/gmail_pop3_with_fetchmail

at any rate, thanks for this part, in particular R. MacGregor & Matthias 
Andree ... i'll be back for more.

just one further thought in the matter of ca-certificates ... actually, 
very little knowledge of the matter was required to get this thing to 
fly. ca-certificates is installed by default in ubuntu. i literally had 
to perform only the steps mentioned above to make this work. while i 
will pursue my study of certificates and digital signatures and so 
forth, no special knowledge was necessary ... except contacting the 
fetchmail forum folks.

i think i'll switch over to a fedora os and try it there.
what are the chances of it being this simple?
