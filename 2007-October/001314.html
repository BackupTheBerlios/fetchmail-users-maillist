SUSE 9.2 is ancient history for fetchmail, and I cannot really claim to
be supporting enterprise distributions - these are usually only
available under support contract or otherwise in exchange for lots of
money (I know CentOS and Ubuntu Server LTS editions are exceptions), so
I don't usually have access, and frankly, at least for the paid
enterprise distros: it's the vendor's responsibility to support these.

My suggestion is to install all needed development packages (sorry, I
can't say which are needed, not even for distributions I know because
I'm not only compiling fetchmail on those machines) and try to build
fetchmail 6.3.8 from source, as Rob suggests. It's probably hard at
first since you need to figure what packages to install for compiling
fetchmail (compiler/gcc, linux-headers, libc development stuff, openssl
+ development stuff, GNU make), but will likely end up with a working
and shiny new copy of fetchmail after the process and compiling updates
should then be quite easy since all the development RPMs are installed.
