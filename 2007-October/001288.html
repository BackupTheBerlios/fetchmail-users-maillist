On 10/6/07, sp4rc <sammyshome at gmx.net> wrote:
> Hello listmembers,
>
> I am running a local postfix+imap constellation in conjunction with
> fetchmail [1] on a gentoo system. If have just recognized that
> fetchmail(?) contructs strange queries. For example pop.gmx.net becomes
> pop.gmx.net.myLocalDomain.com. If I remove "domain myLocalDomain.com"
> from resolv.conf the queries stop. I have already tried the (no) dns
> option but with no luck. Is there a way to prevent this?

That's entirely your resolver, not fetchmail.  Any domain that doesn't
terminate with a '.' is, by definition, incomplete (technically it's
'google.com.', not 'google.com'.  Your resolver is simply appending
the local domain to any DNS queries that don't terminate in a '.',
which is by design.

The solutions are:

1) Append a '.' to the server name, but be aware that any SSL
certificates won't match
2) Don't put domain or search lines in /etc/resolv.conf
