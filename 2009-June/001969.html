>>> [::1/25] failed: Connection refused.

>>> $ sudo lsof -n -i :25 | grep LISTEN exim4 4143 Debian-exim 3u IPv4

>> Untested, but try "smtpname 127.0.0.1"; alternatively, try commenting
>> out the ::1 entry in /etc/hosts.
>
> To be sure that my MTA (Exim4) listens to IPv6 I made
> "dc_local_interfaces='127.0.0.1;::1' " during "dpkg-reconfigure
> exim4-config", and that worked.  In a home network with a router, does
> anybody know whether this is this ok?  Thanks.

I'm unaware of your distribution's exim configuration details (I'm using  
Postfix everywhere, except under Cygwin on Windows XP where IPv6 isn't  
commonplace anyways), but 127.0.0.1 and ::1 are loopback addresses, so  
it's at least safe, meaning not reachable from the outside world (not even  
 from your router).
