Am 28.07.2010, 23:52 Uhr, schrieb Bartłomiej Wołk:

> I have messages on my gmail account that I cannot fetch.
> ($ fetchmail -k) gives my (fetchmail: No mail for USER at gmail.com at  
> pop.gmail.com).

Sorry to hear that.

> When I use mutt to send email to USER at gmail.com from my other account  
> then fetchmail will download only this one message as if the others  
> doesn't exist (web-based client shows them though).

You are not using "--all", so fetchmail is *supposed* to only fetch newly  
arrived mail. The other mail is old/seen already. Seems like this is the  
right thing to do for fetchmail. For lack of trace information, I cannot  
tell if Google supports LAST. If it does, fetchmail will be using it in  
your case.

> My ~/.fetchmailrc file:
> (there are also other accounts but not listed here)
> ----------------------------------
> poll pop.gmail.com with proto POP3 user 'USER at gmail.com' there with
> password 'PASSWORD' is 'SYSUSER' here options stripcr ssl sslcertck
> sslcertpath '/home/SYSUSER/Mail/.certs/' keep mda '/usr/bin/procmail -d  
> %T'
> ---------------------------------

Try adding uidl before user. This will redownload all messages at least  
once if Google adhere to standards.

If that doesn't help, see  
<http://www.fetchmail.info/fetchmail-FAQ.html#G3> for required trace  
information.

As an advance warning, please also note that Google Mail has been known to  
violate standards in the past. Where that is still the case, you'll need  
to report that to Google rather than the fetchmail list, and request that  
the comply with the applicable IETF standards.

> My ~/.procmailrc file:
...

procmail is obsolete. Use maildrop.
